Part 1

  • What is a good working definition of what a System is and perhaps what it is not?

    • A system is a group of components that work together or interact with one another in order to maintain themselves.

  • For the 4 system definations you were to look up please give in your own words what they mean and give an example of them for each case.

    • Modularity - You could move components somewhere else, and they could still do their original purpose
    Decomposibility - The point where something can no longer be broken down without removing the things purpose.
    Emergence - Complex patterns coming from a group (ex, flocks of birds flying, snowflakes falling)
    Chaos Theory - A small cause could cause a large effect (butterfly effect). Ex. 1 person slowing on a highway can cause a traffic jam

  • What is the difference between ideas of Modularity and Tesselation? What are the properties that are in oppostion with each other?

    • Modularity has a purpose and effect whereas the tesselation is the result of repeating geometric shapes.

  • What is the difference between designing something that has Complexity (aperiodic) vs being Uniform (periodic)

    • When something is uniform, something happens X times every X (ex. every 3 squares is a triangle). A consistent pattern with regular intervals

    When something is complex, something does NOT have to happen every X. Has patterns that cannot be easily predicted

  • What is the difference between a system diagram and a some kind of feature diagram like a exploded view drawing or a illustration like this lady bug. Please explain in complete sentances

    • An exploded view is like reverse engineering something where the parts come apart, the something is fully made.

    A system diagram shows how the parts of the system work or interact together.
