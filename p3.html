Exercise 3

As my group was made of only 2 people, we were luckily enough to not have to worry about having too many shapes with the same side. Our common shape ended up being a wave like one as it was very simple but still allowed for interesting possibilities. For myself, a problem I had was making the shape interesting, but I like to think I succeeded in that. I wish I had went with a different shape because I wanted to be able to flip the triangles more ways and have them still connect. When we combined shapes, mine kept ending up in a circular form. The limitations of my partner and I choosing to go with a triangle as the base shape was that it wouldn’t be able to spread out as much as we wanted.

Phase 3

(placeholder) ...Phase 3 of the Tesslation project ....

For this exercise, I wanted to go with a Dovetail lock with key. This kind of fastener was really interesting to me because of the possible keys that would be made to keep the wood together. For my piece, I went with an hourglass shape, with a circular middle as opposed to the 2 large bulbs in normal hourglasses. This fastener would be good for puzzles, but not really much else. Although the shape keeps the wood held together, the fastener/key would break under a lot of pressure (if it was not 2D).

For this exercise, I used simple rectangles with larger or smaller ends in order to find a shape that keeps it together better. This fastener worked out pretty well but there are better alternatives. I could not get pictures but we did have multiple variations.

Phase 8

In the end we decided that we should go with a slotting joint as that ended up being a good fit for our theme. My group and I decided to make it an “anthropological adventure”. The goal of our kit is to reconstruct a prehistoric monster. There are multiple variations of heads, fins, ribs, spines, tails, etc. Each member of the group was given the goal of creating those body part variations. Each time the person would open the kit, they would be able to make a different animal.
